MetaLogic: An Automated Synthesis Framework for Scalable Logical Reasoning

Lukas Helff1,2, Ahmad Omar1, Felix Friedrich1,2, Wolfgang Stammer1,2, Antonia Wüst1,  Tim Woydt1, Rupert Mitchell1,2, Patrick Schramowski1,2,3,4, Kristian Kersting1,2,3,5
1TU Darmstadt  •  2hessian.AI  •  3DFKI  •  4CERTAIN, Germany  •  5Centre for Cognitive Science, Darmstadt

Correspondence: helff@cs.tu-darmstadt.de

Abstract

We introduce MetaLogic, an end-to-end framework for systematic evaluation and training of Large Language Models (LLMs) via Scalable Logical Reasoning. Given a task specification, MetaLogic automatically synthesizes inductive reasoning tasks with precisely controlled difficulty and supplies an executable symbolic judge for deterministic evaluation. Leveraging the framework, we build MetaBench, a 20-level benchmark with 19 000 + prompts that progressively increase in relational, arithmetic, and recursive complexity. Extensive experiments show: (i) LLMs easily produce syntactically valid rules yet struggle to infer correct hypotheses; (ii) recent “reasoning LLMs’’ (OpenAI o1/o3, DeepSeek-R1, Gemini-Flash-Thinking) perform better but at the cost of >15 000 completion tokens per query; (iii) curriculum-based logic-tuning with MetaLogic doubles Llama-3-8B accuracy, reaching parity with state-of-the-art reasoning LLMs while being 10× cheaper in inference. MetaLogic is fully automated, annotation-free, ensures dataset novelty, and provides a scalable environment for probing and advancing logical reasoning in future language models.

Framework Overview

Introduction

Logical reasoning is a fundamental aspect of intelligence, yet modern AI systems still lag behind on tasks requiring robust reasoning and systematic generalization. Existing benchmarks often use multiple-choice questions or rely on other language models for judging, making it hard to verify whether models truly understand logic or merely exploit superficial patterns.

MetaLogic addresses this gap with a symbolic, fully automated pipeline that 1) synthesizes fresh inductive-logic-programming tasks of tunable complexity, and 2) evaluates model outputs through deterministic symbolic execution. With MetaLogic we build MetaBench, a curriculum of 20 levels (22 000 prompts in total) that systematically raise the bar from simple attribute checks all the way to recursive, arithmetic, and relational challenges.
